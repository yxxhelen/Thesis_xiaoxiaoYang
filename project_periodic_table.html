Interactive 3D Periodic Table

Visualizing chemical trends and quantum shells in a 3D pyramid structure.

Overview

This 3D visualization is constructed as an Energy Pyramid, where the spatial arrangement is governed strictly by the four quantum numbers. Unlike traditional 2D tables, this model emphasizes the underlying symmetry of orbital filling and energy level structures.

Design Principles

The pyramid utilizes a sophisticated mapping system to translate quantum mechanics into spatial coordinates:

  • Orbital Block Sectors: The pyramid is segmented into four distinct color-coded blocks corresponding to the azimuthal quantum number \(l\): s (red), p (yellow), d (blue), and f (green).
  • Intra-layer Arrangement: Within each energy layer, the horizontal (\(X\)) and depth (\(Y\)) positions map to the magnetic quantum number \(m_l\) and spin quantum number \(m_s\), respectively. This effectively visualizes the spatial orientation and pairing of electrons within specific subshells.
Physics Context:

The electron configuration follows the: $$n + l \text{ rule (Madelung rule)}$$ mapping atoms into a 3D spatial coordinate system $(r, \theta, z)$.

Interaction: Use Left Click to rotate, Scroll to zoom, and Hover over elements to view detailed quantum properties.
